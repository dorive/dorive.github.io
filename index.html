About Me

Who am I?

Hello, I am David Orive Miguel. I started my studies in Computer Engineering, but despite my love for programming, I felt that my true passions were mathematics and physics. For that reason, I decided to pursue a master's degree in Industrial Mathematics. Since then, I have not separated myself from mathematics, data, and programming! Subsequently, I completed a Ph.D. at the prestigious technological center CEA located in Grenoble, France. I worked on developing mathematical models for tomographic reconstruction in diffuse optics. I secured funding through the Marie-Curie ESR fellowship within the European project BITMAP.

I worked as a Quantitative Trader and Data Scientist at Arfima Trading. At Arfima, I had the opportunity to work on various projects involving different technologies. If I were to summarize my achievements over almost four years, they would include the following projects:

  1. Algorithmic trading project where I develop market-making strategies for STIR futures curves. Using Python and C#, I've created statistical models for pricing these curves. I've also refined and developed the methodology for optimal portfolio acquisition and automated quoting. This project is one of Arfima's flagship initiatives, delving into automated and quantitative trading, leaving behind discretionary decisions.
  2. Statistical analysis of seasonal strategies across various futures markets. Using time series analysis tools, I've developed a statistical methodology to uncover seasonal patterns in financial markets. These strategies allow for portfolio diversification and controlled risk-taking.

Currently, I work as Machine Learning engineer at IKEA within the Digital department.

My professional interests align with applying my knowledge of statistical modeling, machine learning, and software development to ambitious, multidisciplinary projects. I enjoy collaborating with individuals from diverse backgrounds.

In addition to my professional projects, I also have personal projects! You can learn more about them in the Projects section, where you'll find brief descriptions and links to GitHub.

My Approach

How I approach my projects?

When it comes to managing a project, understanding how team members work and the approaches they use is of vital importance. In my case, I consider myself a proactive and results-oriented individual. I am aware that these words are often used superficially. Therefore, I will attempt to describe my work style using two books that have inspired me throughout my professional career:

  • Financial Hacking: Evaluate Risks, Price Derivatives, Structure Trades, and Build Your Intuition Quickly and Easily by P. Maymin. In this book, the author addresses various topics in quantitative finance. What captivates me the most is his approach. He employs a highly practical and industry-oriented perspective. He doesn't get bogged down in mathematical intricacies; instead, he gets straight to the point and provides methodologies for valuing financial products under pressure when intuition is crucial. This is the methodology I employ in my day-to-day work for any job. I get to the heart of the problem and focus on the core issues. I leave secondary technical details for times when there's room to delve into them, allowing me to see the problem as a whole and develop an intuition that helps me unravel what truly matters.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis by J. Tukey. This book lays the foundation for modern data science. It doesn't rely on overly complex methodologies that obscure the reality of data. Instead, it goes straight to the essentials of data analysis (transformations, thoughtful visualization, data treatment, etc.). This book best defines my approach to conducting data science. I focus on the essential and concrete aspects, which then enables me to build more sophisticated methodologies.

PhD in applied mathematics at Commissariat à l'énergie atomique (Grenoble, France) 2016-2019

After completing my master's degree, I went to France to pursue a Ph.D. in applied mathematics and medical imaging. My research involved developing novel algorithms in the field of optical tomography. I had the opportunity to undertake multiple research stays in the United Kingdom, Germany, and Italy. The Ph.D. was funded by a Marie-Curie Fellowship (ESR). My work encompassed:
  • Formulating and developing tomographic reconstruction algorithms based on Mellin-Laplace moments. The algorithms were implemented in Matlab and C.
  • Collaborating on certain implementations of the Finite Element Method in NIRFAST.
  • Creating regularization methods for diffuse optical reconstruction problems.
  • Validating theoretical and algorithmic developments through experiments on humans using silicon photomultiplier sensors.

My doctoral thesis on the development of algorithms for multispectral reconstruction in optical tomography can be found here (73 MB) and also here (web).
